Most people treat AI conversations like disposable text messages. Ask a question. Get an answer. Close the tab. Next time you open it the AI has no idea who you are.

I did this for months before I realized I was introducing myself to the same brilliant colleague every morning and wondering why the output felt generic.

There's a better way. It takes about 30 minutes to set up and it changes everything.

---

## What You're Building

A personal context document that Claude can reference in every conversation. Instead of re-explaining your situation each time you start where you left off. The AI already knows who you are, what you're working on, and how you think.

Think of it like an onboarding doc for a new team member. Except this team member has perfect memory and never needs to be told twice.

---

## Step 1: Write Your Context Document (10 Minutes)

Open a doc. Notion, Google Docs, plain text, whatever you actually use. Cover four things.

**Who you are.** Role, industry, location. Two sentences max.

**What you're working on now.** Top 3 to 5 priorities this quarter. Be specific. Not 'grow the business' but 'launch the new manager development program for Q2 with 40 participants across three offices.'

**How you like to work.** Do you want to be challenged or supported? Do you think in frameworks or narratives? Do you hate bullet points? (I do sometimes.) This part makes the biggest difference and most people skip it.

**Your professional context.** Anything the AI should know about your industry, audience, or domain that would improve its answers. For me this includes the Japanese business context, the L&D landscape, and the fact that I write for multiple platforms with different audiences.

One page. Not more. You can always expand later.

---

> **Building this system forced me to articulate things about my own work I'd never written down. My frameworks. My opinions. My priorities. That was valuable before I even gave it to the AI.**

---

## Step 2: Create Domain Pages (15 Minutes)

Break your knowledge into 2 to 3 domains. For each one capture:

**Frameworks you actually use.** Not the ones from school. The ones you reach for. Kirkpatrick, 70/20/10, MEDDIC, whatever lives in your real toolkit.

**Your opinions.** What do you believe that others might disagree with? These make your AI output sound like you instead of like everyone else. I believe most corporate training doesn't work and that's the first line in my L&D domain page. It sets the tone for everything Claude generates.

**Tasks where you need help.** The recurring stuff where AI would be most valuable. Drafting, research, brainstorming, analysis. Be specific about what "help" looks like for you.

---

## Step 3: Connect It (5 Minutes)

> **1. Claude.ai Projects:** Upload your docs as project knowledge. Every conversation in that project gets your full context automatically. This is the easiest path.

> **2. Claude Code or Desktop with MCP:** Connect Claude directly to your Notion or Google Drive. It reads the docs at session start. More powerful, slightly more setup.

> **3. Any platform:** Paste your context doc at the start of a new conversation. Takes 10 seconds. Changes the output immediately.

---

## Step 4: Keep It Alive (5 Minutes per Week)

Weekly: update priorities and recent decisions. Monthly: review domain pages and evolve your frameworks. After major changes: update immediately.

The system only works if it reflects your actual current reality. Stale context is worse than no context because the AI will confidently give you advice based on last quarter's priorities.

---

With context Claude drafts content that sounds like you. Makes recommendations for your specific situation. Pushes back with relevant counterarguments. Connects dots across your work.

Without context it's smart but generic. With context it's a genuine thinking partner.

30 minutes. Start today. You'll feel the difference in your first conversation.
